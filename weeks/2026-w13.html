⚡ ENERGY SHIFTGlobal / Ceasefire8 Apr 2026
US-Iran two-week ceasefire triggers 20% TTF drop — but structural gas supply damage persists
A two-week ceasefire between the US, Israel and Iran was announced on 8 April, with Iran agreeing to reopen the Strait of Hormuz. TTF initially dropped to €42.50/MWh (its lowest since 2 March) before recovering to the €46–47/MWh range — a ~20% decline from the crisis peak of €61.93/MWh in March. However, analysts caution that the ceasefire removes risk premiums but not structural damage: Qatar's Ras Laffan LNG facility remains 17% offline for an estimated 3–5 years, Europe's gas storage stands at ~28% entering the summer refill season, and the EU has already lowered its storage target to 80%. Brent and WTI fell more than 14–16% on the ceasefire, but remain roughly $20 above pre-war levels. The floor for gas prices is expected to stay above pre-crisis levels due to the refill challenge and lingering supply constraints.
STRATEGIC IMPLICATIONS
→Ceasefire reduces tail-risk but not the structural energy cost disadvantage for European chemicals — TTF at €46/MWh is still elevated vs €35.5 pre-war
→Summer storage refill at ~€46–50/MWh is materially more expensive than 2025 — feedstock and energy costs remain elevated through H2 2026
→Ceasefire is fragile — any resumption of hostilities or Hormuz blockade would send TTF back toward crisis highs rapidly

Five EU countries call for bloc-wide energy windfall tax — DE, IT, ES, PT, AT sign Commission letter
Reuters reported on 4 April that Germany, Italy, Spain, Portugal and Austria formally asked the European Commission to consider an EU-wide windfall tax on energy companies' profits. The stated aim is to finance temporary relief for consumers and curb inflation without adding pressure to public budgets. The Commission confirmed receipt of the letter and said it was assessing it alongside other targeted policy options already under discussion.

EXECUTIVE BRIEF — WEEK 31 MARCH – 9 APRIL 2026
European Chemical Sector: Ceasefire Brings Relief but Not Resolution — Bifurcation Deepens

Overall Assessment: The US-Iran two-week ceasefire announced on 8 April has provided real but conditional relief — TTF dropped ~20% to €46/MWh but remains ~30% above pre-war levels. The structural damage to Qatar LNG supply (17% offline for 3–5 years), Europe's depleted gas storage (~28%), and a severely weakened German chemical confidence signal (Ifo −25.0) confirm the industry is still operating defensively. The key strategic dynamic this week is bifurcation: upstream and energy-intensive chains remain under severe pressure, while specialty and decarbonisation-linked segments continue to attract real investment and innovation.

1. Ceasefire — Conditional Relief, Not Structural Fix
The Iran ceasefire removes the immediate escalation risk premium but leaves the underlying supply problem intact: Ras Laffan needs physical rebuilding over years, European storage needs expensive refilling at elevated prices, and the ceasefire itself is explicitly described as fragile (two weeks only, with Iran raising concerns about violated terms on 9 April). For chemical producers, the floor for energy costs is higher than pre-crisis — €40+/MWh for TTF is the new near-term base case.
2. German Confidence Collapse — Ifo at −25.0
The Ifo index drop to −25.0 (from −16.7 in February) is the sharpest monthly deterioration in German chemical confidence seen in this cycle. Combined with the IGBCE wage deferral and VCI stagnation forecast, all three major labor and industry signals now point in the same direction: prolonged weakness, defensive capex, and rising job-cut risk. Q1 results from BASF, Evonik and Lanxess in coming weeks will be watched closely for any formal rationalisation announcements.
3. Consolidation Completed — Borouge Group International Closes
The completion of Borouge Group International (OMV + ADNOC + NOVA Chemicals) sets a new benchmark for global polyolefin competitiveness: 25% EBITDA margin, 70% low-cost feedstock base, 18% premium on advanced products, and a Vienna secondary IPO on the horizon. This is the most visible and consequential consolidation move affecting Europe's polymer competitive landscape this year. Smaller European polyolefin assets face growing strategic pressure.
4. Innovation Continues — BASF Heat Pump, Syensqo/Shell EO, BASF & Evonik Personal Care
Three innovation signals stand out. BASF's industrial heat pump installation at Ludwigshafen is a tangible site-level decarbonisation investment that sets a replicable template. Syensqo's low-carbon EO partnership with Shell (up to 51% PCF reduction) shows how specialty players are monetizing lower-carbon feedstocks. BASF's biotech bisabolol and biomass-balance UV filter, alongside Evonik's personal care push at in-cosmetics Global, confirm that personal care remains Europe's most commercially active specialty chemistry arena.

Energy — Acute Spike on Middle East Disruption (12 Mar update)
Brent crude surged to ~$98/bbl (intraday high $101.6) and TTF natural gas rose to ~€48.5/MWh on 12 March, driven by the Hormuz Strait disruption cutting ~6M bbl/day of Middle East supply. EU gas storage stands at ~29–31%, well below last year's 40%. The EU is now exploring direct gas-price subsidies or a cap (Reuters, 11 Mar) — a stronger intervention than earlier energy-tax relief discussions. High energy costs remain the primary driver of European chemical plant closures and VCI's 12 March outlook downgrade.

Demand
No clear recovery signal across key end markets (auto, construction, industrial). Chemical demand likely to remain subdued through H1 2026 with any recovery dependent on downstream market improvement.
Feedstocks
Moderate oil prices provide partial offset to cost pressures. US ethane-based crackers (INEOS Project One) will have a significant cost advantage vs European naphtha crackers upon commissioning.
Structural Outlook
The industry is restructuring through a combination of capacity exits, cost programmes and selective investment in future-oriented assets (recycling, hydrogen, specialties). This is a multi-year transition, not a short-term cycle.
