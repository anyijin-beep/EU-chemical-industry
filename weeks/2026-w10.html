VCI cuts German chemical outlook — production to stagnate, recovery unlikely before 2026
Germany's chemical industry association VCI lowered its 2025 outlook on 12 March, now expecting production to stagnate rather than grow by 0.5% as previously forecast. Sales and producer prices are expected to fall by 1%. VCI cited weak demand, high energy costs, supply-chain disruptions, geopolitical risks, tariff threats, and declining German competitiveness. The association said the sector is unlikely to recover before 2026. This is the clearest fresh signal that the European chemical downturn remains structural rather than cyclical.

EXECUTIVE BRIEF — WEEK 5–12 MARCH 2026
European Chemical Sector: Restructuring Phase Deepens, Circular Economy Accelerates

Overall Assessment: The European chemical industry remains in a structural restructuring phase rather than a cyclical recovery. Policy attention is intensifying around energy affordability, carbon-cost design, and demand support for low-carbon production — but the urgency of short-term relief discussions signals how severe the competitiveness gap has become. Corporate investment is narrowly concentrated in circularity, carbon-accounted products, and compliance-driven specialty materials.

0. VCI Downgrade + Gas-Cap Debate — Structural Stress Deepens (12 Mar update)
VCI's cut to stagnation (from +0.5%) and the EU's escalation from energy-tax relief discussions to exploring a direct gas-price cap or subsidy are the strongest new signals this week. Together they confirm that Europe's chemical competitiveness gap is not self-correcting: policymakers are now considering interventionist options not seen since the 2022 energy crisis, and Germany's principal chemical lobby is flagging no near-term recovery. For chemical strategy, this reinforces a prolonged low-growth environment with selective policy relief rather than structural cost normalisation.
1. Policy Momentum: Industrial Accelerator Act + ETS Reform
The Commission's Industrial Accelerator Act (4 March) marks a strategic shift from only penalising emissions to actively creating demand pull for low-carbon European production. Simultaneously, EU governments are pushing for an ETS review by July 2026. Together, these moves signal that carbon-cost design is now a competitiveness issue, not just a climate one. Chemical producers with auditable low-carbon credentials stand to benefit from procurement advantages and reduced power-cost exposure.
2. M&A Fragility: Lanxess/Envalior Deal Collapse
The collapse of Advent's acquisition of Lanxess's ~41% Envalior stake underscores that even well-structured strategic transactions remain vulnerable to financing shocks and risk perception. Portfolio reshaping is still central to European chemical strategy, but deal certainty is weaker. Distressed and sub-scale assets may face longer, more complex sale processes in 2026.
3. Circular Chemistry: Covestro Pyrolysis Pilot + BASF Reduced-PCF Range
Covestro's 2 kt/year smart-pyrolysis pilot with Fraunhofer UMSICHT is one of the clearest circular-isocyanate scale-up signals in Europe — moving from lab concept to industrial demonstration. BASF's launch of reduced-PCF intermediates (BDO, THF, PolyTHF, NMP) shows that lower-carbon drop-in chemistry is becoming commercially standard. Both moves reflect a shift from R&D commitment to competitive positioning.
4. Selective Bright Spots: Amcor Italy + Evonik Methionine Pricing
Amcor's expansion in Lugo di Vicenza for recycle-ready high-barrier films confirms that compliance-driven packaging investment is real and ongoing. Evonik's 10% MetAMINO price increase shows that nutrition/feed-linked specialty chains are using pricing discipline to protect earnings in a way that bulk industrial chains cannot. Neither signal points to broad-based recovery — both point to targeted resilience.

MARKET OUTLOOK — Q1/Q2 2026
Energy — Acute Spike on Middle East Disruption (12 Mar update)
Brent crude surged to ~$98/bbl (intraday high $101.6) and TTF natural gas rose to ~€48.5/MWh on 12 March, driven by the Hormuz Strait disruption cutting ~6M bbl/day of Middle East supply. EU gas storage stands at ~29–31%, well below last year's 40%. The EU is now exploring direct gas-price subsidies or a cap (Reuters, 11 Mar) — a stronger intervention than earlier energy-tax relief discussions. High energy costs remain the primary driver of European chemical plant closures and VCI's 12 March outlook downgrade.

No clear recovery signal across key end markets (auto, construction, industrial). Chemical demand likely to remain subdued through H1 2026 with any recovery dependent on downstream market improvement.
Feedstocks
Moderate oil prices provide partial offset to cost pressures. US ethane-based crackers (INEOS Project One) will have a significant cost advantage vs European naphtha crackers upon commissioning.
Structural Outlook
The industry is restructuring through a combination of capacity exits, cost programmes and selective investment in future-oriented assets (recycling, hydrogen, specialties). This is a multi-year transition, not a short-term cycle.
